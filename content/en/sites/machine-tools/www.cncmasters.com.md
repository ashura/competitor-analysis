## Basic Information

CNC Masters, founded in 1990 and headquartered in Baldwin Park, California (main business operations at 16031 Arrow Hwy Ste J, Irwindale, CA 91706), is a leading manufacturer of CNC milling machines, lathes, benchtop mills, and related automation equipment. Serving both industry and hobbyist markets, CNC Masters offers competitively priced machines including vertical machining centers, rotary tables, waterjet cutters, and computer-aided manufacturing (CAM) software.
(Source: [Metoree](https://us.metoree.com/companies/144855/), [Craft.co](https://craft.co/cnc-masters), [CNC Masters About](https://www.cncmasters.com/about-cnc-masters/))

## Key Features

CNC Masters specializes in CNC mills and CNC lathes, including desktop and benchtop models as well as Bridgeport-type vertical milling machines. Key features include:

- Powerful conversational programming for easy program tweaking
- Vertical Machining Centers (VMC) capable of handling tough materials
- Suitable for both production and prototyping across industries
- Applicable for woodworking, engraving, and metalworking (including steel and aluminum)
- Options for quick tool changers, CNC coolant kits, and R8 collets
- Adjustable jog feed rate and variable spindle speed systems
- User-friendly CNC control and operating software (G Code/M Code)
- Robust stepper motors on X, Y, and Z axes
- Compact footprint ideal for small businesses and hobbyists
- Designed and built in the USA (Irwindale, California)
- Support for both manual and CNC operation
- Conversion kits available for upgrading manual machines
- Accessories such as 4th-axis rotary table, digitizing probe, keypad CNC pendant, and touch screen computer options
- Strong customer support for setup and troubleshooting

(Source: [CNC Masters](https://www.cncmasters.com/))

CNC Masters offers a range of CNC mills, CNC milling machines, and CNC lathes. Featured products include:

## Marketing Focus

CNC Masters targets small businesses, machine shops, startups, DIY CNC enthusiasts, and machinists seeking compact, reliable, and upgradeable CNC solutions. Their marketing emphasizes:

- Bringing manufacturing in-house to maximize ROI, reduce outsourcing, and safeguard trade secrets
- Versatility for prototyping, production, woodworking, and metalworking
- U.S.-built, user-friendly equipment especially for those transitioning from manual to CNC
- Competitive pricing and strong customer support
